Blood Types

There are four main blood types: A, B, AB, and O. These can be positive (+) or negative (-), creating eight possible combinations. Blood type compatibility is important for blood transfusions and organ transplants.

Donations Benefits

Blood donation saves lives by providing essential transfusions for surgeries, trauma, and medical conditions. It boosts community health, reduces the risk of blood-related diseases, and promotes a sense of solidarity. Every donation contributes to a healthier society.

Donation Instructions

To donate blood, ensure you're in good health, eat a nutritious meal, and stay hydrated. Bring identification, and disclose any recent illnesses or medication. A brief medical history will be taken, and a small blood sample tested. The actual donation takes about 10-15 minutes, followed by a rest and refreshments.

How To Donate?

Register on a blood donation website. Wait for calls when blood is needed. Eligibility requires age above 18, good health, and a 6-month gap between donations. Arrive with ID, disclose recent illnesses, and undergo a brief medical history. The process takes about 15 minutes, followed by a rest and refreshments.
